Pete Nielsen

Cartographer. Explorer. Designer.

Sinks Canyon Hillshade

I created this map for a hillshade design challenge. The map is a landcover/hillshade of the Sinks Canyon area of the Wind River Range of mountains in central Wyoming. The "Sinks" is a canyon where the Popo Agie River dives into the mountainside, only to appear at the "Rise" a mile down the road. I have led backpacking trips in this area and wanted to capture the stunning beauty of the arid lowlands and rugged highlands of the Winds. The icons used are custom designs, meant to be associative symbols for those coming to experience the beauty of the Sinks.

Natural Disaster Mapper

This was the final project for an interactive web mapping class. I teamed up with 2 other students to create a way to visualize the impacts of natural disasters in the southwest United States. The project combined all of the skills practiced in the course. NOAA disaster data was processed into a GeoJSON, where it was turned into a proportional symbol map with Leaflet. D3 was used to create a timeline chart for the selected state or county. The search functionality and reclassification of the map involved heavy Javascript usage, culminating in a functioning webpage.

An Empty Feeling Around Town

Done for a group project on vacancy in Madison, this vacancy map is a study in using a map to enhance a larger research project. Using Census data and IndieMapper, I created a simple solution to visualize the relationship between poverty and vacancy in the city. This was my first foray into designing to advance a narrative, not just feature one single piece.

Great American Trails

I created this art map for my final projact in an introduction to Cartography class. The map was created in the style of Cinta Arrabas' map of the Camino de Santiago in Europe. The goal was not to create a reference map of hiking trails, but an art piece that conveys the fun and excitement of through-hiking cross-country.

Isthmus Weather

A simple map also made for Intro to Cartography, this map was created for the challenge of designing a map meant for print in a small space. The print version of this map is 4" x 6", the size that would go into a newspaper. I learned to create isolines with this map, striking the balance between a useful design full of information and a clean design that is readable.

The Refuge

This long-form infographic tackles the challenge of combining the map with other graphic elements to tell a narrative story. I decided to highlight the danger that Arctic drilling poses to caribou herds. As someone who has been to the Alaskan Arctic, I believe strongly in preserving this fragile landscape for its native inhabitants. The challenge of this map involved adding elements of voice, style, and accent to tell a compelling story.
